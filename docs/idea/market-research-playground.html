1The Correction

The single most important finding flips a core assumption from the kitchen-table conversation.

✗ What we believed

"To the best of my knowledge there is no such system in Israel — no product supplied end-to-end, hardware + app." The Israeli/Hebrew/WhatsApp gap is wide open.

✓ What the research found

FamilyOS (by Morad Stern, on base44) is live: Hebrew-native, queried over WhatsApp ("מתי הטסט של הרכב?"), and forwards a class-group message to auto-update the organizer — our exact mechanic. It has no kitchen display, though.

Confidence: HIGH (3-0) on existence · MEDIUM (2-1) on the ~4K-user traction (self-reported, free signups, unaudited). Sources: Mako/N12 Dec 2025, moradstern.com.

3The Wedge vs FamilyOS

AI parsing is table stakes (Skylight, Ohai, Milo all do it). Our defensible edge is four things FamilyOS and the incumbents don't combine.

🖥️ Kitchen-tablet display

Primary wedge

FamilyOS is app + web only. The ambient, shared, always-on kitchen screen — the part Hodaya cares most about — is exactly what they lack. Most concrete differentiator.

🔒 Privacy-first scoping

Trust + legal

Bot sees only explicitly-allowed chats / forwards. Also the only ToS-legal architecture — Hodaya's red line and the safe path converge.

🇮🇱 Deeper localization

Moat depth

Israeli holidays (חגים), gan/school rhythms, white-shirt days, chugim. The harder a US incumbent finds this, the safer we are.

💰 Monetization model

Open

FamilyOS is free — both a threat (price pressure) and a tell (nobody's proven Israeli WTP). Ohai's $9.99/mo is the anchor to test against.
