1Foundation Principle: Nothing Thrown Away

Why foundation-first

One developer, evenings only. The only way that works is if every line written in M1 is still running at the end goal. The webhook service is the trunk; each phase grafts a module onto it (parse.ts, db.ts, transcribe.ts, kiosk/). Two patterns are locked in from day one because they're painful to retrofit: ack-then-process webhooks and the allowlist gate.

5Guardrails Locked From Day One

⚡ Ack-then-process

From M1

Return 200 to Meta immediately, do the work async. Trivial for echo, mandatory once Whisper+Claude take seconds — cheap now, painful to retrofit. Idempotent on wa_message_id (Meta retries).

🔒 Allowlist gate

From M1

Only pre-approved family numbers are processed; unknown senders get a polite refusal. The privacy architecture is the product's trust story — and Hodaya's red line.

🚫 Single-purpose bot

Always

No open-domain chat — family scheduling/tasks only. Keeps us on the ALLOWED side of Meta's Jan-2026 general-purpose-chatbot ban. No training on user data, ever.

6End-Goal Success Criteria

The dogfood month (end of P1) answers one question: did this become a habit? Three measurable gates decide whether we go multi-family.

Forward habit

≥ 1 / day

Forwarding to the bot becomes the default reflex for gan messages, bills, family plans.

Parse accuracy

~80%+

Events land correctly dated without correction. Every correction feeds the golden eval dataset.

Board glances

Daily

The family actually looks at the kitchen display — the form-factor bet (our wedge vs FamilyOS) validated.

If the gates pass

P1 → End Goal: real WhatsApp number + business verification, multi-tenant auth, companion app, opt-in Google Calendar sync, billing. Same parse pipeline, same service — lifted, not rewritten. If they fail: we learned for ~$5/mo, and it still runs our own home.
